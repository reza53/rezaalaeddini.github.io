## The Likelihood Ratio Framework

Forensic DNA interpretation relies on Bayes' Theorem:

$$
LR = \frac{P(E|H_p)}{P(E|H_d)}
$$

## Probabilistic Genotyping

Modern systems like STRmix&trade; model allele peak heights as:

$$
y_i \sim \text{Gamma}(a \cdot x_i, b)
$$

<d-code language="python" editable style="white-space: pre; font-family: 'Courier New', monospace; line-height: 1.6; display: block; padding: 10px; background: #f5f5f5;">
# Likelihood Ratio (LR) Example for Forensic DNA

# Assume the prosecution model gives 90% probability
# and the defense model gives 30% probability
prosecution_prob = 0.90
defense_prob = 0.30

# Calculate the LR
lr = prosecution_prob / defense_prob

# Print the result
print("Likelihood Ratio:", lr)
</d-code>

<d-note>
Thresholds for LR interpretation:
<ul>
  <li>LR > 1,000: Strong support for H<sub>p</sub></li>
  <li>LR < 0.001: Strong support for H<sub>d</sub></li>
</ul>
</d-note>

## Challenges

### 1. Stochastic Thresholds

Low-template DNA requires:

$$
P(y_i > 0 \mid x_i = 0) = 1 - e^{-\lambda}
$$

### 2. Relatedness

Adjusting for kinship:

$$
LR_{\text{related}} = \frac{1 + \theta}{(1-\theta) + \theta \cdot F}
$$
